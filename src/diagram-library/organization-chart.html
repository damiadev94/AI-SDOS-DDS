Organization Chart

Shows the hierarchical structure of an organisation, team, or system — who reports to whom, and how roles are grouped.

Each level represents a tier in the reporting hierarchy. Top-to-bottom flow indicates seniority. Nodes at the same level share the same authority tier.

Notes

Organization Charts are the standard reference for team structure, reporting lines, and ownership mapping. They are also used in software architecture to show component hierarchies, service ownership, and on-call escalation paths. Keep the chart current — stale org charts cause more confusion than having none.
