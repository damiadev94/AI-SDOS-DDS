Mind Map

Organises ideas, concepts, or topics radiating outward from a central theme — useful for brainstorming, note-taking, and knowledge mapping.

Smaller nodes = Sub-branch

A Mind Map radiates from the centre outward. Left and right groups balance the visual weight. Sub-branch nodes are intentionally smaller to indicate hierarchy.

Notes

Mind Maps are excellent for early-stage brainstorming, breaking down a domain into sub-topics, and organising unstructured information. They are non-linear by nature — avoid forcing strict parent-child hierarchies when the relationships are associative rather than structural.
